Privacy Policy

Effective July 10, 2026

The short version: GTray collects no data. The app has no servers, no telemetry, no analytics and no accounts of its own. Everything it stores lives on your Mac.

Who is responsible

GTray is developed by Matías Sánchez Cabrera (Chile). For anything related to this policy, write to matias.sanchez.c@gmail.com.

Data the app collects

None. Your Gmail sessions and cookies, the app's settings and the cached profile pictures are stored locally in your Mac's user data folder, exactly like a browser profile. They are never transmitted to the developer, and the developer has no way to access them.

Where the app's traffic goes

GTray displays Gmail's real web interface, so the app's network traffic goes to Google: the Gmail pages you interact with, and Gmail's own message feed, fetched with your cookies to compute the unread counters. Your use of Gmail is governed by Google's terms and privacy policy. GTray is not affiliated with Google.

The update check

The one request the app makes that isn't Google: once a day it fetches gtray.app/version.json — a plain GET with no identifiers and no cookies — to learn whether a newer version exists. You can turn it off in GTray → Check for Updates Automatically.

This website

gtray.app is a static site hosted on GitHub Pages behind Cloudflare. It sets no cookies and runs no tracking scripts. Cloudflare processes IP addresses as needed to serve and protect the site, and provides us only aggregate, non-identifying statistics.

Third parties

Downloads are served from GitHub Releases and are subject to GitHub's privacy policy. Donations are processed by Ko-fi; we never see your payment details.

Your rights

Since we don't process personal data, there is nothing to access, correct or delete on our side — your data is already only yours. If you have any question or concern, contact us anyway; we'll be glad to help.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, we'll update this page and its effective date. This policy is governed by the laws of Chile.
